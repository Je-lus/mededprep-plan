Two-Track Launch Plan

We're launching CE in two parallel tracks. Track 1 (assessment-only CE) goes live the moment our CAPCE organizational accreditation is finalized — no video required, no per-activity approval queue. Track 2 (video micro-modules) ramps up alongside it. One product sells the other. With organizational accreditation, we publish CE at our own pace — no 2-4 week review bottleneck. The real demand spike is December/January when providers scramble for recertification hours. Our job between now and then: build the catalog, warm the relationships, and be ready when the wave hits.

◆ The Two-Track Strategy Core Strategy ▼
Track 1 — The Razor
Assessment-Only CE
Providers take a 30-question adaptive assessment, review all rationales, receive a diagnostic report showing their knowledge gaps, and earn CAPCE-accredited CE credit. No video. No lecture. The item bank is the product.
  • Price$5 / 0.5 CEH
  • Format30 adaptive questions + mandatory review
  • Time to launchDays after accreditation finalized
  • SourceExisting 10,600+ item bank
  • Capacity~70 distinct topics
  • Key outputDiagnostic report (the aha moment)
Track 2 — The Blades
Video Micro-Modules
Short, focused video modules (15-30 min) recorded by our SME team. When a provider's diagnostic report says "you're weak in pediatric respiratory" — there's a video module for that. The assessment sells the module.
  • Price$3.99-12.99 / module
  • Format0.25-0.5 CEH micro-modules
  • Time to launchRamps month 2-3 as content records
  • Team5-6 SMEs recording + Sydney editing
  • Studio capacity3-5 days/week recording
  • PublishingSelf-accredited (org accreditation)

✎ Track 1: Assessment-Only CE — How It Works First Mover ▼

CAPCE allows CE credit through assessment alone. The educational content is in the rationales. Nobody in EMS is doing this digitally. Pharmacy (ACPE) has the established precedent. We have the item bank, the engine, and the analytics. This launches in weeks, not months.

WHY THIS WORKS
  • CAPCE character count: 30 questions with rationales = ~20,700 chars (2.8x the 7,500 minimum for 0.5 CEH)
  • Item bank must be 30%+ larger than what any individual sees (exposure control for retakes)
  • Randomized questions per attempt — no memorization
  • Review of rationales IS the educational content
  • Pharmacy profession (ACPE) has formal precedent
COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE
  • Zero EMS CE providers offer digital assessment-only CE
  • CE Solutions has "test out" but it's bypass, not standalone
  • Competitors have 10-15 questions per course — we have 10,600+
  • Building a calibrated item bank takes years — we already did it
  • The diagnostic report is something nobody else can generate

⚠ Execution Gaps — Strategy Is Sound, Execution Needs Tightening Action Needed ▼

The strategy is solid. The product is real. The team is capable. These are the "last mile" items that separate a plan from a pipeline.

  • Sales Agency Demos Need to Be Scheduled
    We have warm relationships through Richard, Samantha, the Medical Director, and 100+ EMS program connections. "We know people at agencies" needs to become "we have demos scheduled with agencies." Who owns the outreach? What's the cadence? Target: 5-10 scheduled demos by the time assessments go live.
  • Platform Agency Dashboard Must Be Demo-Ready by October
    Training officers make budget decisions in Q4 for the next year. If we want agency contracts closing in December/January, the diagnostic dashboard needs to be in demos by October/November. This is Jeramey's dev work — needs to be sequenced against CE wrapper and CAPCE integration.
  • Marketing CAPCE Catalog Listing Needs a Landing Page
    Once assessment modules are listed in CAPCE's catalog, providers will discover them organically. Where do they land? It needs to be a clean page that explains what adaptive CE is and lets them purchase. This isn't "SEO work" — it's not having a dead-end when someone clicks through.
  • Process Outreach Cadence & Pipeline Ownership
    Between CAPCE approval and December, someone needs to be converting warm relationships into conversations on a regular cadence. Samantha for Georgia, Heather nationally. Define: how many touches per week? Who tracks the pipeline? How do demos get scheduled?
  • Content Item Bank Mapping to CE Topics
    The 10,600+ items are organized for test prep domains, not CAPCE/NCCP categories. Richard + Jeramey need to map items to CE learning objectives for the first 10-15 assessment topics. The NHTSA taxonomy from the question generation pipeline is the Rosetta Stone for this.
