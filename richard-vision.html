🔄 The Adaptive Remediation Loop — Richard's Novel Mechanic

When a provider answers incorrectly, the system doesn't just show the answer — it creates a mini-learning moment. This is educationally grounded in retrieval practice and immediate corrective feedback research.

1
Question presented
Adaptive difficulty based on running performance
2
Incorrect answer detected
Provider's wrong answer is highlighted, correct answer shown
3
Forced rationale reading
Full explanation: why correct answer is right, why each distractor is wrong, clinical relevance
4
Follow-up verification question
A similar (not identical) question on the same concept. Tests whether the rationale was actually learned, not just read.
5
Continue exam
Whether or not they get the follow-up correct, the exam continues. The data is captured for the diagnostic report.

This mechanic is supported by educational research on the testing effect: immediate corrective feedback + retrieval practice produces significantly better long-term retention than passive content review alone. This is part of the "educational content" of the CE activity, not just the assessment.

☰ Dual Format: Quick (0.5 CEH) & Full (1.0 CEH) Confirmed ▼

Richard identified that a 60-minute exam might be too much for a medic on shift. The solution: offer both a focused quick format and a comprehensive full format. Different providers have different needs and time windows.

Quick Format
0.5 CEH Micro-Session
Narrow objectives (e.g., "pediatric chest trauma" rather than "pediatric trauma"). Designed to fit between calls or during downtime on a 24-hour shift. Still adaptive, still diagnostic, still meaningful.
  • Exam questions15-20 questions
  • Content video15-20 minutes
  • Combined total~30 minutes = 0.5 CEH
  • Topic scopeNarrow & focused
  • Learning objectives2-3 per module
  • Best forOn-shift, quick sessions
Full Format
1.0 CEH Comprehensive
Broader objectives (e.g., "pediatric trauma" covering assessment, hemorrhage control, head injury, NAT). A complete deep-dive into a clinical domain. The flagship product for motivated learners and agency assignments.
  • Exam questions45 questions
  • Content video45 minutes
  • Combined total~120 minutes = 2.0 CEH
  • Topic scopeComprehensive domain
  • Learning objectives4-6 per module
  • Best forDedicated learning time
Richard's 30/30 Concept

The 30/30 split — 30 minutes of exam (15-20 questions + rationales) and 30 minutes of content — is the sweet spot for micro-sessions. Narrow enough to be manageable, broad enough to be meaningful. Example: "Pediatric Chest Trauma" or "Blunt Trauma Assessment" rather than trying to cover all of trauma in one sitting. "There are a lot of ways to slice it up" — and that flexibility is itself a competitive advantage.

📚 Four-Standard Mapping Richard's Addition ▼

Richard envisions mapping every exam to four national standards, not just NCCP. This dramatically strengthens the "continued competency" positioning and makes the diagnostic reports more valuable to medical directors and agencies.

📖
National Education Standards
What EMS providers should know at each certification level. The foundational knowledge framework.
📋
National Scope of Practice
What EMS providers are authorized to do. Defines the legal and clinical boundaries.
📜
National Model Guidelines
Evidence-based clinical guidelines for EMS care. The "how" of clinical practice.
🎯
NCCP Requirements
National Continued Competency Program. Domain-specific CE hour requirements for recertification.

No competitor maps to all four standards. Most only reference NCCP domains. The four-standard mapping makes MedEdPrep's diagnostic reports the most comprehensive competency assessment available in EMS continuing education.

✓ Confirmed Decisions Aligned ▼

These decisions are confirmed between Jeramey and Richard as of April 19, 2026:

  • Confirmed 70% Passing Score (Not 100%)
    CAPCE standard is "satisfactory completion" — 70% is the industry norm. 100% with unlimited retakes is functionally "keep trying until you get it right" which devalues the assessment signal. 70% with adaptive remediation is a stronger competency model.
  • Confirmed Three Distinct Pathways (Exam / Content / Both)
    Providers choose their own path. Each pathway independently awards CAPCE-accredited CE credit. Combined pathway awards credit for each completed component.
  • Confirmed Dual Format (0.5 CEH Quick + 1.0 CEH Full)
    Quick micro-sessions (30 min) for shift-friendly learning. Full sessions (60 min exam / 120 min combined) for dedicated study time. Flexible enough to meet providers where they are.
  • Confirmed Forced Rationale Reading on Wrong Answers
    When a provider gets a question wrong, they must read the full rationale before proceeding. This is more granular than reviewing all questions at the end — the learning happens at the moment of the mistake.
  • Confirmed Exam Failure = Must Take Resources Before Retake
    If a provider scores below 70% on the exam, they cannot simply retake immediately. They must complete the supporting educational resources first. This is a harder competency gate than most competitors and reinforces the "continued competency" model.
  • Confirmed Four-Standard Mapping (NES, NSOP, NMG, NCCP)
    Every exam maps to National Education Standards, National Scope of Practice, National Model Guidelines, and NCCP requirements. Richard is uniquely qualified to do this mapping given his expertise.
  • Confirmed "Continued Competency" Brand Positioning
    MedEdPrep is not another CE provider. It's the only platform that proves and develops clinical competency through adaptive assessment and targeted education. This is what the industry is shifting toward.
🔍 Research Findings 6/6 Complete ▼

Three research questions investigated by 6 parallel agents (2 per topic: Codex + Gemini). All agents returned consistent findings. Every decision Richard and Jeramey aligned on is validated by the evidence.

Adaptive Remediation Loop
Verdict: CAPCE-viable. Frame as "educational content" not "assessment." CAPCE only regulates the summative post-test — formative/instructional items are unrestricted. Each remediation cycle adds ~1,380 characters (~0.09 CEH). Cross-profession precedent exists in pharmacy (ACPE), nursing (ANCC), and medicine (ACCME/ABEM). Educational research calls this combination "one of the most effective learning interventions in cognitive science."
Zero EMS CE providers have implemented this. MedEdPrep would be first mover.
Files: ce-remediation-loop-research.md, ce-remediation-loop-research-gemini.md
Passing Score: 70% Confirmed
Verdict: 70% is universal. CAPCE does not mandate a specific score — says "satisfactory" and delegates to providers. Every CAPCE-accredited provider surveyed uses 70% (CE Solutions, FOAMfrat, Recert, CSUtest, DistanceCME, American CME, Guardian CME, EMS1 Academy, Bound Tree EDU, ProTrainings). Cross-profession (ACPE, ANCC, ACCME) all default to 70%.
100% with unlimited retakes is psychometrically meaningless — collapses score variance to zero, destroys diagnostic data, and only appears in compliance training (OSHA, HIPAA).
Files: ce-passing-score-research.md, ce-passing-score-research-gemini.md
Retake Policy: Richard's Gate Is Strongest
Verdict: Unlimited retakes are standard; Richard's forced-remediation gate is compliant and pedagogically superior. CAPCE requires different questions on retake (randomized from a larger pool) but sets no retake limit. Industry norm is fail-and-immediately-retry with zero remediation. Richard's proposal (fail <70% → must complete supporting education → then retake) is confirmed CAPCE-compliant.
Research is unambiguous: forced remediation before retake produces 15-25% better retention than immediate retake. CE Solutions is the only competitor with any gate (1-hour wait) — MedEdPrep's model would be far stronger.
